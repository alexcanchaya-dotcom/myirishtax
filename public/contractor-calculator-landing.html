Frequently Asked Questions

What is Class S PRSI for contractors?

Class S PRSI is the self-employed PRSI class in Ireland. You pay 4% on income over â‚¬5,000. Unlike employees, Class S doesn't provide unemployment benefits but does count towards State Pension.

When is preliminary tax due?

Preliminary tax for self-employed contractors is due by October 31st each year. You must pay either 90% of the current year's tax liability or 100% of the previous year's tax (whichever is lower).

Do contractors get the PAYE tax credit?

No, self-employed contractors only receive the Personal Tax Credit (â‚¬1,875 in 2024), not the PAYE tax credit. This is one difference between employed and self-employed taxation.

What's the difference between income tax and USC?

Income tax is calculated on profits (income minus expenses), while USC is calculated on gross income before expenses. USC has 4 bands with rates from 0.5% to 8%.

Can I claim pension contributions?

Yes! Self-employed individuals can claim tax relief on pension contributions. The relief is applied at your marginal tax rate (20% or 40%).
