Benefit-in-kind (BIK)

BIK adds the value of certain employer-provided benefits to your taxable income. Use this guide to understand common items and how to reflect them in estimates.

  • Health insurance: employer-paid premiums are usually a taxable benefit.
  • Housing and other perks: accommodation, vouchers above small benefit limits, and some loans can attract BIK.

How to estimate BIK

  1. Identify the benefit type and confirm if exemptions apply (e.g., small benefit scheme).
  2. Calculate the taxable value using the Revenue formula for that benefit.
  3. Add the taxable value to your income and rerun your PAYE/USC/PRSI estimate.
  4. Store evidence for mileage logs, valuation, or invoices in case of queries.

FAQs

Are relocation supports taxable? Some relocation expenses can be exempt; check Revenue concession rules.

Do bike-to-work benefits attract BIK? Approved cycle-to-work schemes are usually exempt up to the statutory limit.

When is BIK reported? Employers generally report through payroll on a real-time basis; self-corrections may be needed for understatements.
