Pension tax relief

Revenue allows relief on qualifying pension contributions subject to age-related limits and an earnings cap. Use this guide to sense-check contributions before filing.

Common limits

  • Relievable contributions are capped by a percentage of earnings that increases with age.
  • An overall earnings cap applies; contributions above the cap do not attract tax relief.
  • Employer contributions can have separate treatment for benefit-in-kind; check plan rules.

How to apply relief

  1. Confirm your age band and the corresponding relief percentage.
  2. Check your relevant earnings against the Revenue cap.
  3. Multiply your eligible earnings by the age-related percentage to find the maximum relievable contribution.
  4. Reduce your taxable income by the relievable amount when estimating PAYE liability.

FAQs

Can I carry forward unused relief? PRSA/Personal pension relief is generally claimed for the year of payment; verify carry-forward rules with your provider or advisor.

Does AVC relief differ? Additional voluntary contributions usually follow the same age-related limits, subject to your overall cap.

How does this affect PRSI or USC? Pension relief reduces taxable income for PAYE, but PRSI and USC bases may differ.
