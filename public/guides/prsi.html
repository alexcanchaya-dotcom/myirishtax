PRSI rates and classes

PRSI applies differently depending on whether you are an employee (Class A) or self-employed (Class S). Use this guide to map the 4% default in the calculator to your situation.

Key reminders

  • Most Class A employees pay 4% PRSI on reckonable earnings.
  • Class S (self-employed) also pay 4% plus potential surcharges and minimum charges.
  • Weekly credits can reduce the effective rate for lower income; these credits are not simulated in the calculator.

How to check your PRSI

  1. Identify your class from your payslip or Revenue correspondence.
  2. Confirm if any PRSI-free weeks or credits apply for low earnings.
  3. Apply 4% to your annual reckonable income for a quick estimate, then adjust for credits as needed.
  4. Enter your income in the calculator with PRSI toggled on to see the baseline deduction.

FAQs

Do pension contributions reduce PRSI? No, PRSI is based on gross reckonable income before pension relief.

Are share-based awards subject to PRSI? Often yes for employees; check the award type and timing.

What about directors? Proprietary directors may fall under different classes; confirm with Revenue or payroll.
