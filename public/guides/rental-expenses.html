Rental income expenses

Landlords can deduct certain expenses to reduce taxable rental profits. This guide summarises common deductions and how to document them.

Allowable expenses

  • Mortgage interest (subject to Revenue rules) and approved RAS/RTB registrations.
  • Repairs and maintenance (not capital improvements).
  • Insurance, management fees, accountancy fees, and letting costs.
  • Wear and tear allowances where applicable for furnishings.

How to apply expenses

  1. Keep invoices and receipts that show the date, supplier, and description.
  2. Separate capital improvements from repairs; capital items may be relieved differently.
  3. Deduct allowable expenses from gross rent to find taxable rental profits.
  4. Include rental profits in your income tax, USC, and PRSI calculations.

FAQs

Can I claim pre-letting expenses? Some pre-letting expenses are claimable within specific time limits; check Revenue guidance.

What about vacant property incentives? Reliefs can vary by scheme; verify conditions before applying.

Do I need to apportion mixed-use costs? Yes, split expenses between private and rental use where relevant.
