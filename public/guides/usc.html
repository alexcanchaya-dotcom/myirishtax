USC rates and thresholds

Use this guide to understand how Universal Social Charge (USC) bands apply and how to map your inputs to the calculator.

Current illustrative bands

  • 0.5% on income up to €12,012
  • 2% on €12,013 – €25,460
  • 4.5% on €25,461 – €55,216
  • 8% on remaining income (surcharge may apply to certain self-employed income)

Always confirm thresholds with Revenue for the relevant tax year.

How to apply USC

  1. Check if your income is below the USC exemption threshold for the year.
  2. Apply each band progressively to your gross income.
  3. Add any applicable surcharge for non-PAYE/self-employed income above the Revenue limit.
  4. Input your total income in the calculator and leave USC toggled on to see the estimate.

Frequently asked questions

Do social welfare payments attract USC? Most social welfare payments are exempt, but taxable benefits may still be included—confirm with Revenue guidance.

What if my income changes mid-year? USC is cumulative; payroll will adjust across the year. The calculator provides a simplified annual view.

Are medical card holders treated differently? Reduced rates can apply; this lightweight guide does not model that relief.
