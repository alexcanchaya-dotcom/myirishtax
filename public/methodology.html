Methodology

How calculations work

Our calculator is designed for speed and clarity. It provides a guide, not advice, and should be paired with our non-advice disclaimer. Thresholds and credits are updated for the latest tax year noted on the homepage.

PAYE logic

  • Applies 20% to income up to the standard rate cut-off you enter, then 40% thereafter.
  • Subtracts the tax credits you provide; defaults use common personal plus PAYE credits for single filers.
  • Does not currently include age, home carer, or rent credits unless you add them to the credits input.

USC logic

  • Uses progressive bands: 0.5%, 2%, 4.5%, then 8% on the remainder unless you toggle USC off.
  • Exemption checks for very low income are not applied; confirm eligibility before filing.

PRSI logic

  • Calculates 4% Class A by default. Self-employed Class S users should validate surcharges separately.
  • Credits for low earnings and part-year exemptions are not simulated in this lightweight tool.

Rounding and timing

  • Outputs are rounded to two decimals for readability; Revenue calculations may round differently.
  • Assumes a full tax year with consistent income; mid-year changes and cumulative bases are out of scope.

What to do next
