Deductible Rental Expenses in Ireland

Reduce your rental income tax by claiming these allowable expenses:

  • Mortgage Interest: 75% of interest paid (not capital repayments)
  • Property Insurance: Buildings and contents insurance premiums
  • Repairs & Maintenance: Fixing damage, painting, general upkeep (not improvements)
  • Management Fees: Letting agent or property management costs
  • Accountancy Fees: Cost of preparing rental accounts
  • Legal Fees: Costs related to letting (not purchase)
  • Advertising: Costs to find tenants
  • Utilities: If paid by landlord (ESB, gas, water)
  • Service Charges: Management company fees for apartments
  • Pre-letting Expenses: Costs before first letting

NOT Deductible

Local Property Tax (LPT) is NOT tax deductible. While you must pay it annually, you cannot claim it as an expense against rental income.

Capital Improvements like extensions, new kitchens, or bathrooms are not deductible (but may reduce Capital Gains Tax when you sell).

Mortgage Capital Repayments are not deductible, only the interest portion.

The ‚Ç¨600 Rental Tax Credit

Irish landlords qualify for a ‚Ç¨600 tax credit against rental income tax. This credit:

  • Applies automatically to all residential rental income
  • Reduces your final tax bill by up to ‚Ç¨600
  • Cannot be claimed if you make a rental loss
  • Is per landlord, not per property

How Rental Income Tax Works

  1. Calculate Gross Rental Income: Total rent received annually
  2. Deduct Allowable Expenses: Mortgage interest (75%), insurance, repairs, etc.
  3. Calculate Net Profit: Gross income minus deductible expenses
  4. Apply Marginal Tax Rate: Tax at 20% or 40% depending on total income
  5. Apply Rental Tax Credit: Reduce tax by ‚Ç¨600
  6. Final Tax Due: Your rental income tax liability

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is only 75% of mortgage interest deductible?

Since January 2019, Irish landlords can only claim 75% of mortgage interest as a deductible expense. The remaining 25% is not deductible. This was a government policy change to reduce tax relief for landlords.

Can I offset rental losses against other income?

No. Rental losses can only be carried forward to offset against future rental profits. You cannot use rental losses to reduce tax on your employment income or other income sources.

Is Local Property Tax (LPT) deductible?

No. LPT is not a deductible expense for rental income tax purposes. You must pay it annually, but you cannot claim it as an expense.

What's the difference between repairs and improvements?

Repairs (deductible) maintain the property in its current state - fixing leaks, painting, replacing broken items. Improvements (not deductible) enhance the property - extensions, new kitchen, adding ensuite bathrooms.

Do I get the rental tax credit if I make a loss?

No. The ‚Ç¨600 rental tax credit only applies if you have a rental profit and therefore owe tax. If you make a loss, there's no tax to credit against.
